This ontology models the international standard NEN-ISO/IEC 19770-1:2018 regarding IT asset management. NEN-ISO/IEC 19770-1:2018 itself provides a framework for implementing and managing IT asset management systems within organizations and is published by the Dutch standards organization NEN. The NEN standard offers terms and definitions for key concepts, including assets, data, hardware, software, and their management. The ontology in turn models these terms and definitions through the use of the SKOS-vocabulary, making these terms interoperable, easily referenceable, and shareable within information technology systems and documentation. This supports the adoption of the NEN-ISO/IEC 19770-1:2018 standard and enhances its integration with other standards and IT asset management practices. Introduction

This RDF-based ontology models the international standard NEN-ISO/IEC 19770-1:2018 for IT asset management. NEN-ISO/IEC 19770-1:2018 helps organizations manage IT assets by providing guidelines for the governance, lifecycle management, and strategic planning of IT assets. Key concepts in the standard include assets, hardware, software, and data management. The ontology itself, although modeling an official standard, has an informal status. It is published by the Dutch Ministry of Finance, with the kind approval of the NEN.

Background

NEN-ISO/IEC 19770-1:2018 is a key standard for organizations aiming to implement or enhance IT asset management systems. It establishes a framework to help organizations manage their IT assets effectively, covering hardware, software, and data throughout their lifecycle. Benefits of adopting NEN-ISO/IEC 19770-1:2018 include improved asset visibility, optimized cost control, risk mitigation, and enhanced decision-making processes. At the same time, organizations want to use and share relevant information pertaining to IT asset management easily across information technology systems, business domains and communities. This requires IT asset management data to be meaningful and interoperable. Semantic web technology can fulfill this requirement.

Objective

The goal of the NEN-ISO/IEC 19770-1:2018 ontology is to provide an interoperable data model of the NEN standard, allowing the terms defined within the standard to be used and shared easily across information technology systems, business domains and communities. The ontology also enables the standard to be linked with other standards, facilitating compliance monitoring, comparative analysis and integration with broader IT asset management frameworks.

Audience

The primary audience for the NEN-ISO/IEC 19770-1:2018 ontology includes IT asset managers, procurement teams, auditors, and IT governance professionals, as well as developers of IT asset management software.

Related work

This RDF-based ontology of the NEN-ISO/IEC 19770-1:2018 standard was created to provide a conceptual fundament for [ITAM](https://minfin-nl.github.io/ITAM/), an IT Asset Management ontology. ITAM provides a standardized vocabulary for modeling the IT Asset Management domain. It defines concepts, classes, properties, and relationships to represent IT assets, their attributes, and their interconnections within the IT Asset Management framework. These components of ITAM are linked to the NEN-ISO/IEC 19770-1:2018 ontology, wherever applicable.

Overview

Description

This ontology captures the terms and concepts used in the international standard NEN-ISO/IEC 19770-1:2018 regarding IT asset management. Each term is represented as a SKOS concept and grouped into a SKOS scheme. Concepts include assets, data, hardware, software, and their associated management processes. The ontology defines these terms with preferred labels, definitions, and identifiers, ensuring ease of use, reference, and sharing across information systems.
